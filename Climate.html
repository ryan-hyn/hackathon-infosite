What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. It is caused by:

  • Rising Temperatures: Increased heat leads to more frequent and severe heatwaves, affecting human health and increasing energy demands.
  • Sea Level Rise: Higher sea levels contribute to coastal erosion, increased flooding, and threaten coastal communities and habitats.
  • Droughts and Flooding: Altered precipitation patterns result in droughts and floods, impacting water resources, agriculture, and infrastructure.
  • Ecosystem Disruptions: Climate change affects biodiversity, leading to shifts in species distributions and ecosystem functions.
